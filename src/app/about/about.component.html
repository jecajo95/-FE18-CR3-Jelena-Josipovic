Ristorante Il Gambero Rosso

PHILOSOPHY

Our philosophy translates into a rediscovery of values ​​and flavors of the past. The roots represent our origins, our history, the foundations that have made us what we are today. Local traditions and the most innovative techniques coexist in our kitchen, there are no rules, there are no limits.

Gambero is housed in a 1900 villa in the heart of Padua, home to the ancient and prestigious Ganzetti furrier's shop. In the dining room, comfortable fabric armchairs as if to recall the idea of ​​a living room at home, because this is how we want our guests to feel, protected by a welcoming, elegant but free from formality environment.

THE CHEF VALENTINETTI

The kitchen in the blood, from the very beginning, as steps of an already marked path…

Born in Padua in 1985, Andrea immediately showed a predisposition for the kitchen. After an initial training at the Abano Terme hotel institute and a series of specialization courses in the gastronomic field, at the age of just 18 he left for the land of origin of his maternal grandparents, the island of Ponza, and experienced his first work experience in a starred restaurant, the Acqua Pazza restaurant of Gino Pesce. The period is short but the lessons learned are many and precious.

In 2004 he returned to Padua and entered, in the following years, in various cuisines of the area. After gaining experience both as a cook and as a pastry chef, one of the most significant moments in his training came in 2006 when he became head of the Alajmo family's Le Calandre restaurant.

A school that forms him as a chef and as a person. Subsequently, in 2008, he moved to Milan at the high-level pastry shop PECK and then began a close collaboration with Denis Dianin at the DeG patisserie in Selvazzano Dentro (PD). In 2014 he won the Gambero Rosso award for best aperitif. At the helm of Radici, beyond prizes and awards, Andrea realizes his project, the one he had been building in his mind for years.
